What is Printing Paste?

**Printing paste** is a viscous mixture consisting of dyes (such as pigments or reactive dyes), thickeners, binders, fixers and other auxiliary chemicals used to apply a print pattern to a textile surface. Printing paste ensures that the pattern remains on the fabric with clear and sharp lines, that the dye adheres to the fiber and that it gains the desired fastness properties.

The consistency of the paste is adjusted according to the application method (screen printing, rotary printing, etc.) and the type of fabric. Correct formulation of the paste is critical for print quality, color yield and production efficiency.

Printing Paste Components and Their Functions:

  • Dyestuff: Pigments or dyes that give the pattern color.
  • Thickener: It prevents the dye from spreading on the fabric and ensures pattern clarity by adjusting the viscosity (consistency) of the paste (e.g. alginate, synthetic polymers).
  • Binder: Polymeric substances that enable pigment dyes to adhere permanently to the fabric surface.
  • Fixative (Catalyst): A substance that accelerates the reaction of the dye with the fiber or the hardening of the binder.
  • Plasticizer: A substance that improves the touch of the fabric by preventing it from hardening after printing.
  • Antipilling Agent: A substance that prevents or reduces pilling.
  • Defoamer: A substance that removes foam formed during paste preparation.
  • Urea: Thanks to its hygroscopic properties, it increases the solubility of the paint and absorbs moisture during steaming.
  • This: It is the main solvent and filler of the paste.

How to Calculate Printing Paste Quantities?

The amount of each ingredient is usually given as a percentage (%) of the total printing paste weight. The calculation is quite simple:

Component Amount (kg) = (Total Printing Paste Weight (kg) * Percentage of Component (%)) / 100

This calculator determines the quantities needed based on the total amount of paste you enter and the percentage of each ingredient separately.
