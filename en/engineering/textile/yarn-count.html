Information About Yarn Count Systems

Yarn count , is an important property that expresses the fineness or thickness of yarn in textiles. Yarn count is usually expressed according to the weight of a given length of yarn (direct systems) or the length of a given weight (indirect systems).

Yarn count is a critical parameter in the production processes (spinning, weaving, knitting), quality control and determining the final product properties. Choosing the right yarn count has a great impact on the strength, handle and overall performance of the fabric.

Direct Yarn Count Systems (Fixed Length, Variable Weight)

  • Tex: The weight of 1000 meters of yarn in grams. The higher the Tex number, the thicker the yarn. (g/1000m) Tex = (Weight (g) / Length (km))
  • Denier: Weight of 9000 meters of yarn in grams. Usually used in filament (synthetic) yarns. The higher the denier, the thicker the yarn. (g/9000m) Denier = (Weight (g) / Length (km)) * 9
  • dTex (decitex): Weight of 10,000 meters of yarn in grams. Used for more precise measurements. (g/10000m) dTex = (Weight (g) / Length (km)) * 10

Indirect Yarn Count Systems (Fixed Weight, Variable Length)

  • Ne (English Cotton Count): It is expressed in the number of 840 yards of yarn weighing 1 pound. The higher the count, the finer the yarn. It is usually used in cotton yarns. (skein/lb) What = (Length (yards) / 840) / Weight (lb)
  • Nm (Metric Number): The length of a yarn weighing 1 gram in meters. The higher the Nm number, the thinner the yarn becomes. It is usually used in wool and synthetic yarns. (m/g) Nm = Length (m) / Weight (g)
