What is the Fiber Mixing Ratio?

Fiber blend ratio refers to the ratio of different types of fibers used in a textile product (yarn, fabric, etc.) to the total mass. This ratio is usually stated as a percentage (%). For example, a 60% Cotton / 40% Polyester blend indicates that 60% of the total fibers are cotton and 40% are polyester.

Blending different fibers is done to obtain combinations of properties that no single fiber can have. This has a direct impact on the performance, feel, cost and aesthetics of the product.

Why is Fiber Blend Made?

  • **Performance Improvement:** For example, the moisture absorbency of cotton can be combined with the durability and wrinkle resistance of polyester.
  • **Cost Optimization:** Reducing cost by blending a more expensive fiber (e.g. wool) with a cheaper fiber (e.g. acrylic).
  • **Touch and Comfort:** Providing certain tactile properties such as a silky feel or softness.
  • **Visual and Aesthetic Properties:** To obtain gloss, matte finish or different painting properties.
  • **Easy Care:** Addition of features such as fast drying and easy ironing.

How to Calculate Fiber Mixture Ratio?

The percentage of each fiber in the mixture is found by dividing the amount of that fiber by the total amount of fiber and multiplying by 100.

Fiber A Percentage (%) = (Fiber A Amount / Total Fiber Amount) * 100

This calculator finds the total amount by adding up all the fiber amounts you entered and then presents the percentage of each fiber that makes up that total.
