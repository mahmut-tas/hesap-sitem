What is Mass Balance?

Mass balance , is based on the principle of conservation of mass in a system. According to this principle, mass cannot be created or destroyed in a system. Simply put, the total mass entering a process system must equal the total mass leaving the system. This is a fundamental tool for designing, analyzing and optimizing processes in chemical engineering.

Mass balances are commonly used in continuous flow processes, batch processes, and semi-batch processes. Mass balances can be written for each component in a system, either individually or for the total mass, whether reactions are taking place or not.

How to Calculate Mass Balance?

The simplest mass balance equation is:

Input Mass = Output Mass + Accumulation

In steady-state systems (systems that do not change with time), the accumulation term is assumed to be zero. In this case, the equation simplifies to:

Input Mass = Output Mass

Our calculator uses this basic principle to produce the sum of two given input streams as a combined output stream. This can be used in simple mixing or reactionless separation processes.
