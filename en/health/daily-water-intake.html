What is Daily Water Requirement and Why is it Important?

**Daily Water Requirements** is the estimated amount of fluid your body needs each day to function properly. Water is a critical element responsible for regulating body temperature, transporting nutrients and oxygen to cells, protecting organs, and removing waste products from the body. Drinking enough water helps keep your energy levels high, your digestive system functioning properly, and your skin healthy.

The body is made up of approximately 60% water, and we constantly lose water through sweating, urinating and breathing. Drinking water regularly is vital to replace these losses, prevent dehydration and maintain overall health.

How to Calculate Daily Water Requirement?

One of the most common methods of calculating daily water needs is to use a formula based on body weight. It is generally recommended that an adult consumes an average of 30-40 ml of water per **kg**. For example, for a person weighing 70 kg, the calculation is as follows:

Daily Water Need = Kilogram (kg) × 30-40 ml

  • Lower limit for 70 kg: 70 kg × 30 ml/kg = 2100 ml (2.1 liters)
  • Upper limit for 70 kg: 70 kg × 40 ml/kg = 2800 ml (2.8 litres)

This means that a person weighing 70 kg should drink an average of 2.1 to 2.8 litres of water per day.

Factors Affecting Water Need:

While the formula above is a general guide, individual water needs can vary depending on many factors:

  • **Physical Activity Level:** People who exercise intensely lose more water through sweating and need more water.
  • **Climate:** Living in hot and humid climates or at high altitudes increases the need for water.
  • **Health Conditions:** Conditions such as fever, vomiting, diarrhea or some chronic diseases (e.g. kidney diseases) may affect water needs.
  • **Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:** Pregnant and breastfeeding women&#39;s water needs increase.
  • **Nutrition:** People who consume high amounts of fruits and vegetables also get some water from food.

This calculator is a general estimate. It is important to consult a healthcare professional for the most accurate assessment of your personal water needs. Do not change your water intake significantly without talking to your doctor, especially if you have any health conditions or take regular medications.
