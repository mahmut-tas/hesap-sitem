This calculator calculates the number of bricks or blocks required for a given wall area, the volume of mortar, and the quantities of cement, sand, and water required for the mortar. Factors such as wall and brick/block dimensions, joint thicknesses, and wastage rates are taken into account. Standard options or custom inputs are available for mortar proportions.

Wall Area = Wall Length × Wall Height

1 Brick + Joint Area = (Brick Length + Horizontal Joint) × (Brick Height + Vertical Joint)

Number of Bricks Required = (Wall Area / 1 Brick + Joint Area) × (1 + Brick Waste Rate / 100)

Mortar Volume = Number of Bricks × Brick Volume per Mortar Volume (see JavaScript for detailed calculations)

  • Wall Area: Yapılacak duvarın toplam yüzey alanı ($m^2$).
  • Number of Bricks/Blocks: Total number of bricks or blocks required for the wall.
  • Mortar Volume: Duvar örme için gerekli toplam harç hacmi ($m^3$).
  • Mortar Materials: Harç için gerekli çimento (kg), kum ($m^3$) ve su (litre) miktarları.
  • Waste Rate: Percentage excess added to compensate for material losses.

Important Note: Brick/block dimensions, joint thicknesses and mortar mix ratios may vary depending on the project specifications, local standards and type of materials to be used. The default values in this calculator are general assumptions. A structural engineer, craftsman or material supplier should always be consulted for exact quantities to be used in real projects. Density and moisture content of materials may also affect final quantities.

Why is Brick/Block Wall Material Calculation Important?

Accurately calculating the amount of materials needed before building a wall is vital to the **success, cost control and time management** of the project. Under-ordering leads to downtime, delays and additional costs, while over-ordering leads to unnecessary waste and storage issues. This calculator will help you determine exactly what you need, allowing you to stay on budget and keep your project running smoothly.

Mortar Rates and Material Requirements:

Mortar is the basic material that binds bricks or blocks together and is usually made up of a mixture of **cement, sand and water**. Lime is sometimes added. The strength and workability of the mortar depends on the proportions of these ingredients. This calculator provides default values for common mortar proportions:

  • Cement: It is the binding and strength-giving component of the mortar. It is usually calculated in kilograms (kg).
  • Sand: Harcın dolgu maddesidir ve işlenebilirliğini, dayanımını ve hacim stabilitesini etkiler. Genellikle metreküp ($m^3$) cinsinden hesaplanır.
  • This: It ensures the hydration of the cement and the consistency of the mortar. It is usually calculated in liters.

Different mortar ratios are used for different wall types and load-bearing capacities. For example, 1:3 mortar (1 part cement, 3 parts sand) is more durable, while 1:5 mortar is less durable but more economical. Our calculator gives you estimated material quantities based on the mortar ratio you choose.

Importance of Fire Rate:

During the use of bricks/blocks and mortar on a construction site, some loss is inevitably experienced. This is called **waste**. The waste rate is a percentage added to the calculated amount to cover these losses. Causes of waste may include:

  • **Brick/Block Waste:** Breakage during transportation, cutting, dropping or installation.
  • **Mortar Waste:** Spillages and disposal of excess mortar during mixing, transportation or application.

Generally, 3-5% waste ratio is used for brick/block and 5-10% for mortar. However, these ratios may vary depending on the quality of workmanship, type of material and site conditions.

Application Areas:

  • Garden Walls and Fences: Material planning for wall elements in landscape projects.

  • Cost Estimate: Accurately estimating material items when creating a construction budget.

This calculator provides general estimates and includes simplified assumptions. Factors such as the shape (hollow/solid) of the bricks/blocks, their porosity, the moisture content and specific gravity of the aggregates, the consistency of the mortar and the quality of workmanship can significantly affect actual material requirements. For large or critical projects, it is vital to determine exact proportions and quantities in consultation with material suppliers or qualified civil engineers.
